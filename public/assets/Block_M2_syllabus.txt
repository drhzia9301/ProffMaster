Based on the EYE MODULE (4th Year MBBS) syllabus provided, here are the subjects and topics organized by theme.

Theme 1: Foundation of Ophthalmology

Basics & Physiology

Visual standards and blindness (WHO classification).

Anatomy and physiology of visual pathway.

Visual field defects.

Pupil reflexes (Normal and Abnormal).

Visual functions (Acuity, color vision, contrast sensitivity, light brightness).

Optics & Refraction

Refraction, Pseudophakia, Aphakia, and Anisometropia.

Refractive Errors: Myopia, Hypermetropia, Astigmatism, Presbyopia (Pathophysiology, clinical presentation, and management).

Pharmacology

Drugs used in common eye diseases.

Investigations

Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT).

Visual fields (VF).

Fundus Fluorescein Angiography (FFA).

Ultrasonography (B-Scan).

Biometry and Corneal Topography.

Theme 2: Lid Abnormalities & Bulging Eyes

Eyelid Disorders

Lid Bumps: Chalazion, Stye (Pathophysiology and management).

Tumors: Tumors of Eyelids.

Positions/Movements: Ptosis (Causes, assessment, management).

Structural: Trichiasis, Entropion, Ectropion.

Orbit & Neuro-Ophthalmology

Proptosis: Etiology and management in children and adults.

Infections: Preseptal and Orbital Cellulitis.

Thyroid Eye Disease (TED).

Systemic: Myasthenia Gravis, Migraine.

Theme 3: Red Eye

Conjunctiva & Sclera

Conjunctival inflammations (Bacterial, Viral, Fungal, Allergic).

Pterygium and Pseudo-Pterygium.

Episcleritis and Scleritis.

Cornea (Keratitis)

Bacterial Keratitis (Corneal ulcers).

Fungal, Viral, and Acanthamoeba Keratitis.

Non-infectious corneal inflammations.

Lacrimal System & Lids

Dacryocystitis (Acute, Chronic, Acute on Chronic).

Congenital nasolacrimal duct obstruction.

Blepharitis.

Dry Eyes (Sjogrenâ€™s syndrome, Vit. A deficiency).

Uvea

Uveitis (Anterior and Posterior).

Ocular Trauma

Open Globe Injury (OGI): Intraocular Foreign Body (IOFB), Sympathetic Ophthalmia.

Closed Globe Injury (CGI): Orbital Floor Injury.

Chemical/Physical: Radiation, Thermal, and Chemical injuries.

Visual Rehabilitation.

Theme 4: Visual Loss

Glaucoma

Aqueous humor dynamics and IOP.

Open Angle: Primary Open Angle Glaucoma (POAG), Normal Tension Glaucoma (NTG), Ocular Hypertension (OHT).

Angle Closure: Primary Angle Closure Glaucoma (PACG), Acute angle closure.

Secondary: Neovascular Glaucoma, Lens Induced Glaucoma.

Treatment options (Medical and Surgical).

Lens

Cataract (Age-related, pathogenesis, complications, management).

Surgery complications: Endophthalmitis, Panophthalmitis.

Cornea

Keratoconus (Ectasia).

Corneal dystrophies and degenerations.

Retina & Choroid

Vascular: Diabetic Eye Disease (Retinopathy/Maculopathy), Hypertensive Retinopathy, CRVO, CRAO.

Detachment: Retinal Detachment (RD).

Oncology: Choroidal Melanoma.

Dystrophies/Nutritional: Retinitis Pigmentosa, Vitamin A deficiency (Night blindness).

Neuro-Ophthalmology

Optic Neuritis.

Papilledema vs Disc swelling.

Hereditary, Nutritional, and Toxic Optic Neuropathies.

Theme 5: Childhood Blindness & Crossed Eyes

Pediatric Ophthalmology

Leukocoria (White Pupil): Differential diagnosis, Retinoblastoma.

Congenital Cataract.

Congenital Glaucoma.

Amblyopia (Lazy eye).

Childhood Blindness causes and management.

Strabismus (Squint)

Concomitant: Esotropia, Exotropia.

Incomitant (Paralytic): 3rd, 4th, and 6th nerve palsies.

Diplopia (Double vision).
