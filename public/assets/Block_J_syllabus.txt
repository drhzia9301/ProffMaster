Based on the NEUROSCIENCES-II MODULE (4th Year MBBS) syllabus provided, here are the subjects and topics organized by theme.

Theme I: Disturbed Sleep

Psychiatry

Sleep disorders (Types and management)

Non-organic insomnia

Sleep wake cycle disorders

Pharmacology

Introduction to CNS Pharmacology: Neurotransmitters, BBB, Ion channels, Common terminologies (tolerance, dependence).

Sedative-hypnotics (Minor tranquilizers): Benzodiazepines, Barbiturates, Buspirone, Ramelteon.

CNS Stimulants: Respiratory analeptics, Methylxanthine, Sibutramine.

Forensic Medicine

Classification of neurotoxins

Cerebral Poisons (Somniferous): Morphine, Opium, Heroin.

Inebriant Poisons: Ethyl Alcohol, Methyl Alcohol.

Sedative & Hypnotics: Chloral hydrate, Barbiturates.

Fuels, Stimulants, and Hallucinogens: Agrochemical poisons, Kerosene, LSD, Amphetamines.

Drug Dependence.

Community Medicine / Epidemiology

Epidemiology concepts

Study Designs

Screening

Measures of mortality and morbidity

Theme II: Disturbed Mood & Behaviour

Psychiatry

Mood & Anxiety: Depressive disorders, Bipolar Affective Disorder, Suicide, Anxiety Disorders, Dissociative disorders, Stress related disorders, Somatoform disorders, Atypical depression, Seasonal affective disorder.

Psychotic Illnesses: Personality disorders, Psychotic disorders, Schizophrenias, Delusional disorders, Substance abuse disorder.

General Medicine

Alzheimer`s disease and Dementias.

Pharmacology

Depression: Antidepressants (SSRIs, TCAs, MAOIs).

Psychoses: Antipsychotics (Anti-schizophrenic drugs).

Bipolar Affective Disorder: Mood-stabilizing drugs (Lithium carbonate).

Alcohols: Ethanol, Methanol poisoning.

Opioids: Morphine, Diamorphine, Codeine, Tramadol, etc.

Drugs of abuse: Addiction, Habituation, Club drugs, Nicotine/Cannabis abuse.

Forensic Medicine

Insanity and relationship to criminal charges (McNaghten rules, Durham`s rule).

Forensic Psychiatry (Mental health act, Admission/Discharge procedures).

Testamentary capacity (Will).

Civil and criminal responsibility of mentally ill patients.

Community Medicine

Mental health (Epidemiology, Risk Factors, Prevention).

PRIME / Medical Education

Conflict resolution.

Community Medicine / Biostatistics

Introduction to Biostatistics (Variables, Sampling, Biases).

Theme III: Right-sided weakness and inability to speak

Pathology

Hypoxia, ischemia, and infarction.

Intracranial haemorrhage.

Strokes syndromes.

Subarachnoid haemorrhage (SAH).

General Medicine

Stroke (Types, Risk factors, Management of infarction and bleed).

Community Medicine

Non-communicable diseases: Strokes (Epidemiology, Prevention, Rehabilitation).

Neurosurgery

Neurosurgical management of stroke and Subarachnoid hemorrhage.

Community Medicine / Biostatistics

Measures of central tendency.

Measures of dispersion.

Normal distribution.

Confidence Interval, Confidence level, Standard error.

P value, critical region, rejection region.

Theme IV: Loss of Consciousness and Fits

General Medicine

Seizures (Tonic-Clonic, Absence, Psychomotor, Status epilepticus).

Anaesthesia

Introduction to Anaesthesia (General, Regional, Neuroaxis block).

Preoperative evaluation and risk assessment.

Monitoring in anaesthesia.

Pharmacology

Anti-seizure drugs: Carbamazepine, Phenytoin, Valproate, Ethosuximide, Phenobarbitone, Benzodiazepines, Lamotrigine, Topiramate.

Status epilepticus management.

General Anaesthetics: Inhaled anaesthetics, IV anaesthetics.

Pre-anaesthetic medications.

Obstetric analgesia.

Forensic Medicine

Deliriant Poisons: Dhatura, Hyocyamus nigra, Cannabis indica.

Community Medicine / Biostatistics

Z test & its application.

Types/shapes of frequency distribution.

Theme V: Tremors

Pathology

Neurodegenerative disorders: Alzheimers disease, Parkinsons disease, Huntington’s Disease, Spinocerebellar ataxias, Motor Neuron disease.

General Medicine

Parkinson`s disease.

Motor Neuron disease.

Pharmacology

Drug-induced Parkinsonism.

Paediatrics

Cerebellar ataxias (Friedreich’s Ataxia).

Community Medicine / Biostatistics

“t” test & its application.

Chi square test & its application.

Correlation, regression.

Practical Problems in biostatistics.

TBM (Tuberculous Meningitis).

PRIME / Research

Data analysis (MS Excel, SPSS, Endnote).
