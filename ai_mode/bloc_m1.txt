Based on the ENT MODULE (4th Year MBBS) syllabus provided, here are the subjects and topics organized by theme.

Theme I: Sore Throat

Anatomy & Physiology

Anatomy of oral cavity, pharynx (nasopharynx, oropharynx), and deglutition.

Anatomy and physiology of adenoids and pharyngeal tonsils.

Anatomy and physiology of major (parotid, submandibular, sublingual) and minor salivary glands.

Diseases of the Pharynx & Oral Cavity

Pharyngitis: Acute and Chronic (Classification, etiology, clinical features, management).

Tonsillitis: Acute and Chronic.

Abscesses: Peritonsillar abscess (Quinsy).

Oral Ulcers: Aphthous ulcers and differential diagnosis.

Trauma: Injuries to the palate and oropharynx (Soft tissue and bone repair).

Tumors: Carcinoma of the oral cavity.

Salivary Gland Disorders

Non-neoplastic disorders (Sialadenitis).

Sialolithiasis (Stones) and Sialectasis.

Neoplasms (Benign and Malignant tumors).

Complications of salivary gland surgeries.

Theme II: Difficulty in Swallowing

Esophageal Disorders

Dysphagia: Causes, Anatomy/Physiology of Esophagus, Medical/Surgical treatment.

Plummer Vinson Syndrome: Predisposing factors and management.

Pouches: Pharyngeal and Esophageal pouches.

Tumors

Oropharyngeal Tumors.

Hypopharyngeal Tumors.

Tumors of Esophagus (Classification, etiology, management).

Theme III: Hoarseness & Stridor

Anatomy & Physiology

Larynx: Applied anatomy, potential spaces (Pre-epiglottic, paraglottic, Reinke’s space), Physiology of voice/speech.

Neck Spaces: Deep fascia, Peritonsillar, Parapharyngeal, Submandibular, Retropharyngeal spaces.

Laryngeal Disorders

Inflammation: Acute and Chronic Laryngitis (including granulomatous conditions).

Vocal Cord Lesions: Vocal nodules, Vocal polyps.

Neurological: Vocal cord paralysis (Unilateral/Bilateral).

Tumors: Malignant Tumours / Carcinoma of Larynx (Epidemiology, UICC classification, Management).

Airway Obstruction & Trauma

Stridor: Congenital, Acquired (Apyrexial/Pyrexial).

Acute Respiratory obstruction (Signs, Management).

Tracheostomy: Indications and procedure.

Laryngeal trauma.

Laryngotracheal Foreign bodies.

Theme IV: Deafness, Ear Discharge & Dizziness

Anatomy & Physiology

External, Middle, and Internal ear.

Functions of the ear.

Tuning fork tests.

Pure Tone Audiometry (PTA) and Impedance audiometry.

External Ear & Trauma

Trauma to External Ear and Temporal Bone.

Otitis Externa.

Middle Ear Diseases

Acute Suppurative Otitis Media (ASOM).

Chronic Suppurative Otitis Media (CSOM): Tubotympanic (without cholesteatoma) and Atticoantral (with Cholesteatoma).

Complications: Intracranial and Extracranial complications of SOM.

Mastoiditis: Acute and Chronic.

Hearing Loss & Rehabilitation

Conductive Hearing Loss (CHL): Otosclerosis, Otitis Media with Effusion (OME).

Sensorineural Hearing Loss (SNHL): Tinnitus, Ototoxicity.

Deafness: Congenital vs Acquired, Approach to a deaf patient/child, Rehabilitation.

Inner Ear & Balance

Vertigo: True vertigo, Pathophysiology.

Vestibular Neuronitis.

Meniere’s Disease.

Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo (BPPV).

Tumors: Acoustic Neuroma.

Theme V: Nasal Obstruction

Anatomy & Physiology

Surgical anatomy and physiology of Nose & Paranasal Sinuses (PNS).

Congenital disorders (Choanal atresia).

Nasal Septum & Polyps

Deviated Nasal Septum (DNS).

Sino-Nasal Polyposis.

Inflammation & Infections

Rhinitis: Allergic, Non-allergic, Infective (Acute/Chronic).

Sinusitis: Acute and Chronic, Fungal Rhinosinusitis, Complications of sinusitis.

Granulomatous Diseases: Nose and PNS.

Tumors

Sino-Nasal Tumors (Benign and Malignant).

Juvenile Nasopharyngeal Angiofibroma.

Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma.

Trauma & Miscellaneous

Trauma to Nose and Face (Le Fort fractures).

CSF Rhinorrhea.

Headaches and Facial Pain (Rhinogenic).

Foreign Body, Rhinolith, Maggots in Nose.

Epistaxis (Management approach).

Adenoids (Diseases and treatment).

Theme VI: Neck Swelling

Infections

Parapharyngeal Abscess.

Retropharyngeal Abscess.

Submandibular Abscess.

Trauma

Trauma of the Larynx and Neck.

Oncology & Clinical Approach

Evaluation of metastatic lymph nodes.

Occult Primary in Head and Neck.

Approach to a neck swelling.

Clinical Schedule (Practical Skills)

History Taking: Sore throat, Hoarseness, Stridor, Deafness, Ear discharge, Dizziness, Nasal obstruction.

Examinations:

Ear (Otoscopy, Tuning fork tests, Balance testing, EUM).

Nose (Anterior/Posterior rhinoscopy, Patency, Probe test).

Throat (Oral cavity, Oropharynx, Tonsils).

Neck (Lymph nodes, Thyroid, Parotid, Lumps).

Larynx/Pharynx (Indirect laryngoscopy/hypopharyngoscopy).

Procedures & Skills:

Nasendoscopy (Observation/Assist).

Swab taking (Pus culture/Sensitivity).

Biopsy taking (Oral, Laryngeal, Nasopharyngeal).

Tracheostomy (Demonstration).

Pre-operative informed consent (Tonsillectomy, Laryngectomy).

Scrubbing technique.

Interpretation of Investigations:

Audiograms and Tympanograms.

X-Rays (Nasopharynx/PNS).

CT Scans (Nose/Nasopharynx).
